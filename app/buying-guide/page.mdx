# Skateboard Buying Guide

Building your first skateboard or upgrading your current setup? This guide covers everything you need to know about our skateboard components and how to choose the right gear for your riding style.

## Skateboard Decks

We offer two main deck types to match your skating style:

### Street Decks

Premium 10-ply Canadian maple construction in three widths:

- **8.0":** Best for technical tricks and smaller riders
- **8.25":** All-around size, most versatile for street and park
- **8.5":** More stability for transition skating and larger riders

**Graphic options:** Choose from Flames, Geometric, Abstract, Skull, or Minimalist designs to match your style.

### Longboard Decks

Hybrid maple and fiberglass construction in three lengths:

- **38":** Compact and nimble, great for city cruising
- **41":** Mid-length all-arounder for cruising and light downhill
- **44":** Maximum stability for downhill and long-distance riding

**Mounting styles:**
- **Drop-Through:** Lower center of gravity, easier pushing, great for beginners
- **Top-Mount:** More responsive turning, better for tricks and slides
- **Drop-Down:** Lowest platform, maximum stability for speed

**Flex ratings:**
- **Stiff:** Best for downhill and heavier riders (180+ lbs)
- **Medium:** All-around flex for most riders (140-180 lbs)
- **Flexy:** More comfortable ride for lighter riders and cruising (under 140 lbs)

## Wheels

Choose your wheels based on what and where you'll be skating.

### Street Wheels

Designed for park and street skating with excellent grip and slide control:

- **50mm:** Maximum pop and control for technical tricks
- **54mm:** All-around size for street and park
- **56mm:** Faster rolling while still trick-friendly

**Durometer options:**
- **99A:** Hard wheels, fast, best for smooth surfaces and tricks
- **101A:** Very hard, maximum speed for park skating
- **83B:** Softer option (~99A equivalent), smoother on rough streets

**Profile shapes:**
- **Classic:** Balanced shape for all-around performance
- **Bighead:** Wider contact patch for more stability and grip

### Cruiser Wheels

Large, soft wheels for smooth cruising and carving:

- **65mm:** Nimble and maneuverable for city cruising
- **70mm:** All-around size for cruising and light downhill
- **75mm:** Maximum speed and roll for long-distance and downhill

**Durometer:**
- **75a:** Super soft, smoothest ride over rough terrain
- **78a:** Standard cruiser hardness, great all-around
- **83a:** Slightly firmer, faster roll with good comfort

**Types:**
- **Cruiser:** Rounded edges for smooth carving and city riding
- **Downhill:** Square-lipped for maximum grip at speed

## Trucks

Match your trucks to your deck width and riding style.

### TKP Trucks (Traditional Kingpin)

For street decks and technical skating:

- **129mm:** Pairs with 8.0" decks
- **139mm:** Pairs with 8.0"-8.25" decks
- **144mm:** Pairs with 8.25"-8.5" decks

**Construction options:**
- **Standard:** Solid, affordable, great for most riders
- **Hollow:** Lighter weight without sacrificing strength
- **Forged:** Premium construction for maximum strength and response

### RKP Trucks (Reverse Kingpin)

For longboards:

- **144mm:** Pairs with 38" longboards
- **149mm:** Pairs with 38"-41" longboards
- **159mm:** Pairs with 41"-44" longboards

**Construction options:**
- **Standard:** Reliable cast construction
- **Forged:** Stronger and more precise
- **Precision:** CNC-machined for the ultimate in performance but very expensive.


## Bearings

**Ball type:**
- **6-ball:** Standard configuration, reliable and affordable
- **7-ball:** More balls = smoother roll and longer life

**Material:**
- **Steel:** Durable and affordable, great for most riders
- **Ceramic:** Less friction, faster roll, rust-resistant

**Spacer type:**
- **Classic:** Traditional separate spacers
- **Built-In:** Integrated spacers for easier maintenance

**Recommended combinations:**
- Budget: 6-ball Steel Classic
- Mid-range: 7-ball Steel Built-In
- Premium: 7-ball Ceramic Built-In

## Grip Tape

**Width options:**
- **9":** Standard for most street decks
- **10":** For wider decks (8.5"+)
- **11":** For extra-wide decks and longboards

**Grit options:**
- **Standard 60 Grit:** All-around grip for most skating
- **Extra Coarse 80 Grit:** Maximum traction for aggressive skating

**Color:**
- **Black:** Classic look, hides dirt
- **Clear:** Show off your deck graphic

## Complete Setup Recommendations

### Street Skating Setup
- 8.0" or 8.25" Street Deck
- 54mm Street Wheels (99A or 101A)
- 139mm TKP Trucks (Standard or Hollow)
- 6-ball or 7-ball Steel Bearings
- 9" Standard Grip Tape

**Est. Price Range:** $150-210

### Park/Ramp Setup
- 8.25" or 8.5" Street Deck
- 54mm or 56mm Street Wheels (101A)
- 139mm or 144mm TKP Trucks (Forged)
- 7-ball Steel Bearings
- 9" or 10" Extra Coarse Grip Tape

**Est. Price Range:** $170-240

### Cruising/Commuting Setup
- 38" or 41" Drop-Through Longboard (Medium flex)
- 70mm Cruiser Wheels (78a)
- 149mm RKP Trucks (Standard or Forged)
- 7-ball Steel Built-In Bearings
- 11" Standard Grip Tape

**Est. Price Range:** $210-340

### Downhill Setup
- 41" or 44" Top-Mount Longboard (Stiff flex)
- 70mm or 75mm Downhill Wheels (78a or 83a)
- 149mm or 159mm RKP Precision Trucks
- 7-ball Ceramic Built-In Bearings
- 11" Extra Coarse Grip Tape

**Est. Price Range:** $340-550

## Pro Tips

- Match your truck width to your deck width for best performance
- Softer wheels = smoother ride; harder wheels = faster and better for tricks
- Don't cheap out on trucks - they last the longest and affect your control the most
- Ceramic bearings are worth it if you skate frequently or in wet conditions
- Extra coarse grip tape wears out faster but provides maximum traction
- Consider your weight when choosing longboard flex rating
